General Regulatory Terms

A

Accreditation
A formal process by which an authorized body assesses and recognizes an organization, program, group, or individual as meeting established requirements or standards.
Administrative Agency
A government entity created by law to administer and enforce regulatory frameworks governing public programs or professions.
Administrative Law
The body of law that determines how regulatory bodies operate and the scope of their authority, including statutes, rules, regulations, orders, and decisions.
Administrative Penalty
A financial or non-financial sanction imposed by a regulatory agency for non-compliance with laws or regulations.
Adjudication
A legal process by which a court or authorized body resolves a dispute or decides an issue based on evidence presented.
Advisory Opinion
A non-binding statement issued by a regulatory body providing guidance on how rules or laws may apply to specific situations.
Allegation/Complaint
A formal claim or assertion that an act, event, or result has (or has not) occurred, often leading to an investigation by the regulatory body.
Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR)
Methods used to resolve conflicts outside of formal adjudication, such as mediation or arbitration.
Appeal
A request for a higher authority to review and reconsider a decision made by a regulatory body or court.
Appellate Body
A higher-level body or committee that reviews decisions made by a lower-level regulatory body or committee.
Audit
A systematic review or examination of records, processes, or practices to verify compliance with regulations or standards.
Autonomous Agency
A government agency that operates independently, usually with its own statutory authority and governance structure, and is not directly controlled by a ministry or department. These agencies often have significant discretion in their operations and decision-making.

B

Best Practice
A method or technique that has consistently shown results superior to those achieved with other means, and thus used as a benchmark.
Board
A group of individuals appointed to oversee, regulate, and make decisions regarding a profession or industry.
Bylaws
Rules adopted by an organization for its internal governance and operation.

C

Case Management
The process of organizing, tracking, and overseeing complaints or investigations from initiation to resolution.
Certification
The process by which an authorized body recognizes an individual, institution, or program as meeting specific standards or requirements.
Cease and Desist Order
A directive from a regulatory body requiring an individual or organization to stop engaging in a specific activity.
Charter
A document outlining the purpose, structure, and powers of an organization or regulatory body.
Code of Conduct
A set of rules outlining the ethical and legal duties and obligations of members of a profession or organization.
Code of Ethics
A set of principles and guidelines intended to help professionals conduct their business honestly and with integrity.
Competency Assessment
A process used to evaluate whether a professional has the necessary knowledge, skills, and abilities to practice safely and effectively.
Compliance
The act of following laws, regulations, guidelines, and specifications relevant to a profession or organization.
Conflict of Interest
A situation in which a person's private interests could improperly influence their official duties or decisions.
Conflict Resolution
The process of resolving a dispute or disagreement between parties, often through mediation or negotiation.
Consent Order
A formal agreement between parties, approved by a regulatory body or court, to resolve a complaint by taking specific actions (e.g., ceasing an activity, paying a fine).
Continuing Competence
The ongoing demonstration that a professional maintains the necessary knowledge, skills, and abilities to practice safely and effectively.
Continuing Education
Ongoing professional development required to maintain licensure or certification.

D

Declaration of Interest
A formal statement in which an individual discloses any personal, financial, or other interests that could influence their official duties.
Delegated Authority
The power granted by a regulatory body to another entity or individual to perform certain regulatory functions.
Delegation of Authority
The act of assigning decision-making powers to another person or group within the organization.
Disciplinary Action
Measures taken by a regulatory body in response to professional misconduct or non-compliance, such as warnings, fines, or suspension.
Disciplinary Committee
A committee responsible for reviewing complaints, conducting hearings, and recommending disciplinary actions.
Due Diligence
The investigation or exercise of care that a reasonable person would undertake before entering into an agreement or transaction.
Due Process
The legal requirement that regulatory bodies must follow fair procedures and respect individuals' rights when making decisions that affect them.

E

Enforcement
The process of ensuring that individuals or organizations follow the laws, regulations, and standards set by regulatory bodies.
Ethics Committee
A group responsible for reviewing ethical issues and providing guidance on ethical conduct within the organization.
Ex Officio Member
A member of a board or committee by virtue of their office or position, rather than through election or appointment as a regular member.
Ex Parte Communication
Communication between a decision-maker and one party without the knowledge or participation of other parties, generally discouraged in regulatory proceedings.
Examination
A formal test or assessment used to determine if an individual meets the qualifications required to practice a profession.

F

Fair Hearing
A legal proceeding in which all parties have an opportunity to present evidence and arguments before an impartial decision-maker.
Fiduciary Duty
A legal obligation to act in the best interest of another party, especially in financial or trust matters.
Freedom of Information (FOI/FOIA)
Laws or policies that provide the public with access to government records and information.

G

Good Governance
The set of policies, processes, and behaviors that ensure an organization operates in a transparent, accountable, and ethical manner.
Good Standing
A status indicating that a professional or organization is compliant with all regulatory requirements and is authorized to practice or operate.
Governance
The act, process, or power of exercising authority or control within an organization, including setting policies and ensuring accountability.

I

Impaired Professional
A professional whose ability to practice safely is compromised, typically due to substance abuse, illness, or other factors.
Impartiality
The principle that decisions should be made without bias or favoritism.
In Camera Session
A private session, closed to the public, often used for discussing sensitive or confidential matters.
Inquiry
A formal process to investigate a specific issue or complaint.
Interim Order
A temporary order issued by a regulatory body to address urgent issues while a full investigation or hearing is pending.
Investigative Report
A formal document summarizing the findings of an investigation into alleged misconduct or non-compliance.

J

Jurisdiction
The official power or authority of a regulatory body to make, interpret, and enforce laws or regulations within a defined area or over a specific group.

L

Legal Counsel
An attorney or law firm providing legal advice and representation to the regulatory body.
Legislative Framework
The collection of laws and statutes that provide the legal basis for regulatory activities.
Legislative Oversight
The periodic review of government agencies by a legislative committee or division to ensure proper administration and accountability.
License/Registration
Official recognition by a regulatory body that an individual or organization is qualified and authorized to practice a profession or operate a business.
Licensure by Endorsement
A process by which a regulatory body grants a license to an individual already licensed in another jurisdiction, based on certain criteria.

M

Mandate
The official authority or mission of a regulatory body as defined by law or policy.
Mandatory Reporting
The requirement for certain professionals to report specific incidents (such as abuse or misconduct) to the appropriate authorities.
Minutes
A written record of the proceedings and decisions made during a meeting.
Moral Character Requirement
A standard used to assess whether an individual's past conduct and behavior make them suitable to practice a profession.

N

Non-Compliance
Failure to meet legal or regulatory requirements.
Notice of Hearing
A formal notification to all parties involved about the time, place, and subject of a hearing.

O

Ombudsman
An official appointed to investigate complaints against public authorities and seek resolutions.
Open Meeting
A meeting conducted in public, allowing for transparency and public participation.

P

Parliamentary Procedure
Rules for orderly conduct of meetings, often based on Robert's Rules of Order.
Peer Review
A process in which professionals within the same field evaluate each other's work for quality, accuracy, or compliance with standards.
Policy
A set of principles or rules adopted by a regulatory body to guide decision-making and operations.
Policy Development
The process of creating, reviewing, and updating policies that guide the organization's operations.
Procedural Fairness
The principle that all parties involved in a regulatory process are treated justly and given a fair opportunity to present their case.
Procedural Rules
Guidelines that govern the processes and conduct of meetings, hearings, and other official activities.
Professional Member
A member of a board, committee, or regulatory body who is a licensed or certified professional in the field being regulated.
Promulgate
To officially issue and publicize a law, rule, or regulation, making it effective.
Proposed Rule
A regulation published by an agency for public review and comment before adoption.
Provision
A requirement or stipulation set forth in a statute or regulation.
Public Comment
The process of gathering feedback from the public on proposed laws, rules, or regulations before they are finalized.
Public Member
A member of a board or committee who is not a licensed professional in the field being regulated, often representing the public interest.
Public Interest
The welfare or well-being of the general public, often a guiding principle in regulatory decisions.
Public Record
Information or documents that are available for public inspection.

Q

QA (Quality Assurance)
Processes ensuring that products or services meet specified requirements.
QC (Quality Control)
Procedures to ensure that a product or service adheres to defined quality criteria.
Quasi-Judicial
Activities in which a regulatory body acts like a court, making judgments and issuing decisions that affect the rights and obligations of individuals or organizations.
Quorum
The minimum number of members required to be present to conduct official business.

R

Recusal
The act of withdrawing from participation in a decision due to a conflict of interest.
Registrar
The official responsible for maintaining records and administering regulatory processes.
Regulation
A rule or mechanism established by a government or other authority to control, guide, or limit behavior within a profession or industry.
Regulatory Framework
The structure of laws, regulations, and policies that govern a profession or industry.
Reinstatement
The process of restoring a license or registration after it has been suspended or revoked, typically following specific requirements.
Remedial Action
Steps taken to correct non-compliance or address issues identified during an investigation.
Revocation
The removal of a license or registration, prohibiting an individual or organization from practicing a profession or operating a business.
Risk Assessment
A process used to identify, evaluate, and prioritize risks to public safety or professional standards.
Risk Management
The process of identifying, assessing, and controlling risks to the organization.
Rules of Evidence
Guidelines that determine what evidence is admissible in hearings or legal proceedings.

S

Sanction
A penalty imposed by a regulatory body as a result of a disciplinary process.
Sanction Guidelines
Policies that outline the types and severity of sanctions that may be imposed for violations.
Secretariat
The administrative office or staff that supports the activities of a regulatory body.
Scope of Practice
The range of responsibilities and activities that a professional is permitted to perform, as defined by law or regulation.
Semi-Autonomous Agency
A government agency that operates with a degree of independence from direct political control but is still ultimately accountable to a parent ministry or department. Semi-autonomous agencies often have their own governing boards and decision-making authority within defined limits.
Self-Regulation
A system in which members of a profession themselves, rather than an external body, set and enforce standards of practice.
Standard of Practice
The minimum level of performance expected from professionals to ensure safe, high-quality services.
Statute
A law enacted by a legislative body that governs conduct within its scope.
Statutory Authority
The legal power granted to a regulatory body by legislation to make and enforce rules and regulations.
Statutory Obligation
A duty or responsibility required by law.
Subpoena
A legal order requiring a person to appear before a regulatory body or court to provide testimony or evidence.
Sunset Review
A periodic review process to determine whether a regulatory body or program should continue, be modified, or be terminated.
Sunshine Law
Laws requiring government agencies to conduct business in public view.
Supervision
Oversight of professionals or organizations to ensure compliance with regulations.

T

Task Force
A temporary group formed to address a specific issue or project.
Transparency
The principle of being open, clear, and accountable in regulatory processes and decision-making.
Tribunal
A body established to adjudicate disputes or appeals.

U

Umbrella Agency
A larger administrative organization that oversees and coordinates multiple smaller agencies or departments, providing centralized support and sometimes governance.

W

Whistleblower
An individual who reports misconduct, fraud, or illegal activities within an organization.

US-Specific Terms

Advance Notice of Proposed Rulemaking (ANPRM/ANPR)
A document issued by an agency before a formal rule proposal, seeking public input on potential regulatory changes at an early stage.
Administrative Procedure Act (APA)
A federal law establishing the procedures federal agencies must follow when making regulations, including requirements for public notice and comment.
Authorizing Statute (Enabling Statute)
A law passed by Congress that grants authority to an agency to regulate a specific area and may outline procedural requirements for rulemaking.
Code of Federal Regulations (CFR)
The codification of the general and permanent regulations promulgated by executive departments and agencies of the US federal government, divided into 50 subject-matter titles.
Federal Register
The official daily publication for rules, proposed rules, and notices of US federal agencies and organizations, as well as executive orders and other presidential documents.
Federalism
The relationship between the federal government and state, local, and tribal governments, including issues of preemption and consultation.
Information Collection Request (ICR)
A formal request by a federal agency to collect information from the public, subject to approval under the Paperwork Reduction Act.
Informal Rulemaking
A process under the APA involving notice and comment, used for most federal regulations.
Office of Information and Regulatory Affairs (OIRA)
An office within the Office of Management and Budget that reviews federal regulations and information collection requests for compliance with laws and executive orders.
Office of Management and Budget (OMB)
A federal agency that oversees the implementation of the President's vision across the Executive Branch, including regulatory review and federalism oversight.
Organic Statute
A law that establishes a federal agency and its authority to issue regulations; also known as an enabling statute.
Paperwork Reduction Act (PRA)
A law requiring federal agencies to obtain approval before collecting information from the public and to minimize public burden.
Regulatory Flexibility Act (RFA)
A law requiring federal agencies to assess the impact of regulations on small businesses and consider alternatives that minimize economic impact.
Unfunded Mandates Reform Act (UMRA)
A law limiting the ability of federal agencies to impose unfunded mandates on state, local, or tribal governments.

Canadian-Specific Terms

Agreement on Internal Trade (AIT)
An intergovernmental agreement aimed at promoting labour mobility and mutual recognition of credentials among Canadian provinces and territories.
C.R.C. (Consolidated Regulations of Canada, 1978)
The official compilation of federal regulations in force in Canada as of 1978.
DAO/DAA (Delegated Administrative Organization/Authority)
A corporation that, through an administrative agreement with a ministry, is responsible for administering an act and associated regulations on behalf of the provincial government.
Professional Code (Quebec)
The umbrella statute in Quebec that sets out which occupations are deemed professions and the basic regulatory framework for all professions.
Provincial Jurisdiction
The constitutional authority for professional regulation lies with the provinces in Canada, not the federal government.
R.S. (Revised Statutes)
Refers to revised statutes, such as the Revised Statutes of Canada (1985) or earlier, which are updated compilations of federal laws.
SI (Statutory Instruments)
A formal legal document made in exercise of a statutory power.
SOR (Statutory Orders and Regulations)
The official way to refer to regulations that are made by the Governor in Council under the authority of an Act of Parliament.

EU-Specific Terms

Adverse Event
Any untoward medical occurrence in the context of a clinical investigation or product use.
CE Mark (ConformitÃ© EuropÃ©enne)
A mandatory conformity marking for certain products sold in the European Economic Area, indicating conformity with health, safety, and environmental protection standards.
Clinical Trial (CT)
Any investigation in human subjects intended to discover or verify clinical effects or safety of investigational products.
Competent Authority
The national body responsible for implementing and enforcing EU regulations in each member state (e.g., for medicines, medical devices).
Conformity Assessment
The process of demonstrating whether the requirements of a regulation relating to a product have been fulfilled.
Conformity Assessment Body
An organization that performs third-party conformity assessment activities, such as testing, certification, and inspection.
Decision
A binding act addressed to specific parties, such as a country or company.
Directive
A legislative act that sets out a goal for EU countries to achieve, leaving it to each country to determine how to reach it.
MDR (Medical Devices Regulation)
Regulation governing medical devices in the European Union.
Notified Body
An organization designated by an EU country to assess the conformity of certain products before being placed on the market (notably for medical devices).
Regulation (EU)
A binding legislative act that must be applied in its entirety across the EU.
