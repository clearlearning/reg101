⏱️ The Application Timeline

Click each step to explore the typical licensure application process. Follow Maria's journey as she applies for her nursing license!

1

Complete Required Education

Maria graduates from an accredited nursing program

What happens: Maria completes a Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BSN) at State University. The program is accredited by the appropriate nursing education accrediting body.

Why it matters: Graduation from an accredited program ensures Maria has received education that meets professional standards. Her transcript will be verified directly by the licensing board.

2

Submit Application & Documents

Maria applies and provides required documentation

What Maria submits:

  • Completed application form
  • Official transcripts (sent directly from university)
  • Application fee payment
  • Passport photo and ID verification
  • Self-declaration form about criminal history

Processing time: Typically 2-4 weeks for initial review.

3

Credential Verification

The board verifies Maria's education is authentic

What happens: The nursing board contacts State University directly to verify that Maria completed her BSN program, the dates of attendance, and that the degree was properly awarded.

Additional checks: The board verifies the program was accredited at the time Maria attended. If Maria had attended school outside the country, this would involve additional credential evaluation services.

Timeline: 1-3 weeks depending on institution response time.

4

Criminal Background Check

Suitability assessment through background screening

What happens: Maria submits fingerprints for a criminal background check. The board receives results showing any criminal convictions.

Assessment: The board reviews any findings to determine if they relate to Maria's suitability to practice nursing. Not all criminal history automatically disqualifies someone—the board considers the nature, severity, and time elapsed.

Maria's case: Her background check comes back clear with no criminal history.

5

Pass Licensing Examination

Maria takes and passes the NCLEX-RN exam

The examination: Maria takes the standardized test that assesses the knowledge, skills, and abilities essential for safe, effective nursing practice.

Format: Computerized adaptive test that adjusts difficulty based on Maria's responses. She must demonstrate minimum competency across all areas of nursing practice.

Results: Maria receives her results within 48 hours and has passed!

6

License Issued!

Maria receives her nursing license and can begin practice

Final step: With all requirements met—education verified, background check complete, and examination passed—the nursing board issues Maria her license.

What Maria receives:

  • License number
  • License certificate
  • Confirmation that she's listed on the public registry
  • Information about renewal requirements

Total time from application to license: Approximately 8-12 weeks.
